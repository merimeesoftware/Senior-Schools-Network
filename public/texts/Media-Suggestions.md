# Media Suggestions

A curated selection of films and shows that embody wonder, adventure, and Catholic formation. These recommendations align with the philosophy of poetic knowledge — emphasizing beauty, virtue, heroism, and the natural world.

## Movies

### Adventure & Heroism

- **The Lord of the Rings Trilogy** (Peter Jackson, 2001-2003): Epic quest embodying sacrifice, friendship, courage, and the battle against evil. Rich in mythic imagination and moral clarity.
- **The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe** (2005): Allegorical journey of redemption through sacrifice, wonder, and the triumph of good over evil.
- **Robin Hood** (1938, Errol Flynn): Classic swashbuckling adventure celebrating justice, loyalty, and chivalric virtue.
- **The Man Who Would Be King** (1975): Kipling's tale of adventure, hubris, and the price of imperial ambition in remote lands.
- **Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World** (2003): Naval adventure emphasizing leadership, duty, friendship, and the demands of honor.

### Nature & Wonder

- **March of the Penguins** (2005): Documentary revealing creation's beauty through the sacrificial love and perseverance of emperor penguins.
- **Planet Earth** (BBC series): Visual poetry of the natural world, inspiring awe and wonder at God's creation.
- **Winged Migration** (2001): Breathtaking documentation of bird migration patterns, showcasing the marvels of instinct and creation.
- **The Secret of Roan Inish** (1994): Gentle Irish tale of family, folklore, and the mystery of the sea.

### Family & Virtue

- **The Sound of Music** (1965): Music, family, courage in the face of tyranny, and the beauty of the Austrian landscape.
- **Chariots of Fire** (1981): True story of athletes balancing competitive excellence with faith and principle.
- **It's a Wonderful Life** (1946): Classic American film celebrating community, sacrifice, and the value of an ordinary life well-lived.
- **Babette's Feast** (1987): Danish film exploring grace, art, sacrifice, and the sacramental nature of a feast.

### Historical & Literary Adaptations

- **A Man for All Seasons** (1966): St. Thomas More's principled stand against tyranny, conscience over convenience.
- **The Mission** (1986): Jesuit missionaries in South America — faith, sacrifice, and the clash of civilizations.
- **Persuasion** (1995, BBC): Jane Austen adaptation emphasizing constancy, virtue, and redemptive love.
- **Pride and Prejudice** (1995, BBC miniseries): Austen's masterwork brought to life with wit, social insight, and moral formation.

## TV Shows & Series

### Family-Friendly

- **The Waltons** (1972-1981): Family life during the Great Depression, emphasizing faith, hard work, and rural community.
- **Little House on the Prairie** (1974-1983): Pioneer resilience, family bonds, and moral lessons rooted in frontier life.
- **Anne of Green Gables** (1985, CBC miniseries): L.M. Montgomery's imaginative orphan navigating friendship, family, and virtue.

### Documentary Series

- **Civilisation** (1969, Kenneth Clark): Magisterial exploration of Western art and culture from the Dark Ages to modernity.
- **The World at War** (1973): Comprehensive World War II documentary, essential for historical understanding.
- **Blue Planet** (BBC): David Attenborough's exploration of ocean life, inspiring wonder and stewardship.

### Historical Drama

- **I, Claudius** (1976, BBC): Roman history brought vividly to life, morally complex but historically rich.
- **Downton Abbey** (2010-2015): English manor life across WWI and social change, exploring class, duty, and virtue.

## Animated Films

### Classic & Imaginative

- **Spirited Away** (2001, Hayao Miyazaki): Japanese masterwork of imagination, courage, and spiritual themes.
- **The Prince of Egypt** (1998): Biblical exodus story rendered with reverence, music, and visual splendor.
- **Sleeping Beauty** (1959, Disney): Classic fairy tale with stunning stylized animation and timeless themes.
- **Fantasia** (1940, Disney): Classical music paired with imaginative animation, cultivating wonder and aesthetic delight.

## Cautionary Notes

- **Screen Time Balance**: Media should supplement, not replace, outdoor play, reading, and imaginative games.
- **Age Appropriateness**: Preview content for younger children; some historical films contain mature themes.
- **Active Viewing**: Discuss themes, virtues, and questions arising from films to deepen understanding.

## For Further Exploration

This is a sampling from a larger curated list. These selections prioritize beauty, virtue, adventure, and wonder — foundational for poetic formation. Use them as starting points for family viewing and discussion.
